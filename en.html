La leche prometida

La leche prometida is an investigation cross-border of Ojo Público that analyzes the contracts of the Vaso de Leche program of Peru with NIISA, its main provider. This special has 2 visualizations with which I collaborated.

Explore the interactive web here.

Frosts in the highlands

Frosts in the highlands is an interactive web that explains how indigenous communities located in the upper and lower areas of Peru deal with the consequences of climate change, in addition to the lack of government support and the loss of their main livelihoods.

Explore the interactive web here.

Otros arbitrajes, mismas trampas

Otros arbitrajes, mismas trampas is an interactive web that discovers, details and compares the arbitral corruption perpetrated by Brazilian companies of the Lavajato Cartel in Peru. This interactive web has 2 visualizations with which I collaborated.

Explore the interactive web here.

#Betetadas

#Betetadas Is a website that collects real-time tweets from Fujimorista parliamentarian Karina Beteta, who is known for her controversial opinions such as qualifying people - critics of her and her party - of "terrucas, reds or caviars."
This website connects to Google Spreedsheats and Workbench data.

Explore the interactive web here.

Mapa relacional CNM

The mapa relacional CNM is a website developed with D3.JS that finding links to the characters involved in the corruption scandal CNMAudios. In addition to the links, you can read a small description of who they are and what they do. The map is adaptable to various devices if nodes and links are regulated. It also has a search engine in case you have a name in mind.

Explore the interactive web here.

Especial LavaJuez

LavaJuez is a website developed for IDL-R whose main objective is to be a repository of the investigation they did to uncover corruption in the Judiciary, better known as CNM Audios. On the platform you can search for the characters involved and listen to their respective audios, read articles related and learn about the research methodology.

Explore the interactive web here.

La epidemia invisible

La epidemia invisible it's a website developed for IDL-R whose main objective is to show in an interactive and intuitive way the investigation carried out by the journalist Rosa Laura about the HIV outbreak in the jungle of Peru.

Explore the interactive here.

Golpe a la prensa

Golpe a la prensa is an interactive website that recalls the coup d'etat of former dictator Alberto Fujimori. It is narrated from the point of view of how that event affected press freedom. It has an interactive infographic, a simulation of the edition of La Republica newspaper which was censored, and videos of testimonies from journalists.

Tortura y muerte en Los Cabitos

Tortura y muerte en Los Cabitos is an interactive website that collects testimonies of families affected by the tortures and extrajudicial executions that occurred in 1983 during the Alan García government. You can also find statistics and related documents.

Más de 200 mil personas son esclavos en el Perú [DATA]

Más de 200 mil personas son esclavos en el Perú [DATA] is an investigative report on the statistics of precarious and forced labor in Peru, compared with different Latin American countries. To produce this report, we extrapolated databases from the Public Ministry, the World Bank and the Walk Free Foundation.

Explore the interactive news here.

El otro gran show

El otro gran show is a platform that analyzes how the private life of the characters of the Peruvian show has become a profitable business and how people consume these contents.

Explore the interactive web here.

El juicio final: la matanza en el Frontón

El juicio final: la matanza en el Frontón is an interactive website that serves as a repository of victims and perpetrators of the killing in the El Frontón prison, located near the coast of Callao, which occurred in 1986 during the first government of Alan García.

The social role of journalism is communication between government and citizens. In short, if we had no more journalism, it would be an incredibly difficult task to control and investigate power.

This is important if we analyze the modern history of the current socio-economic system, where the accumulation of goods and capital is impregnated in the collective imaginary as an intrinsic right to individual freedom, as a place where the chances of corruption are latent in when there is an opportunity for the appropriation of the public good by the private one.

Good journalism is then called to safeguard the principles that make us human and remind us in the midst of so many ideologies, post truth, fake news, manipulation of public opinion, sectoral and personal interests, etc., yes, that our way of thinking is conditioned to how society is organized but that the main task is not to reproduce the right and wrong, the bad and the good, but to analyze the facts as consequences, keeping ourselves independent as far as possible and taking the position of serving The society in a positive way.

It has taken me time to learn that politics is a life decision. That it is, not only to exercise every 5 years our right to participate in elections, but to participate daily in the decisions we make between us for the good coexistence in society. How we interact, how we organize ourselves, how we manifest ourselves, how we act collectively, how we become a state and a nation.

In this sense, politics is in every area of ​​our existence. From what we wear to what we say or stop saying.

For example, feminism, a movement that fights for the liberation of women, is an intrinsically political movement, because it is about revolutionizing the way we build our lives and how we recognize ourselves in front of others. When we understand it in this way, we understand, for example, that musical genres that denigrate and / or minimize our bodies like reggaeton are part of the problem.

We also understand that we shouldn't talk about recovery of rights for some and not for all women, so feminism can't want to establish itself in the current socio-economic system in which we live because it's a system that generates inequality gaps so high that 1% of the rich accumulate 82% of global wealth. and that therefore being a feminist becomes a kind of social justice activism as well.

Another recent but increasingly important movement as a governance philosophy is transparency and access to public information. In times where technology is mimicked in our lives with greater force, we can realize that all the time we generate and consume information and that our data becomes a new currency.

Therefore, we can use this logic to identify in our society the problems to be solved and their causes if we begin to measure our individual and collective interaction; it's mean the data generated in our citizen exercise. For example, when boarding a bus or visiting a park.

On the other hand, budgets, visit registration, number of contracts, redistribution of works and goods, etc. which are public information generated from the state and the public service, it helps citizens to contribute to a free and informed public opinion and thus control power.

Therefore, it's necessary to demand access to public information in reusable formats and it's necessary to recognize it as a political movement that becomes a right as it's a new form of protest and gives us a greater probability of increasing our citizen presence.
